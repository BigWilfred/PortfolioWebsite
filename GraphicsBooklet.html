Graphics Booklet

A correlation of work completed independent in the Graphic Design course. Contained within the booklet were two distinct tasks competed over the course:

1. Five Fact Illustrations: Each fortnight, we were given a new list of unrelated facts. The task was to word these facts in an informative way and convey this meaning through an illustration. The individual pieces were critiqued and revised.

2. Infographic: A large A2 poster on a subject of your choosing. Responsible with finding relevant statistics, designing all graphics and sourcing an adequate printer. Challenged with illuminating the most interesting pieces of information in a simplistic manor.

The process of producing highly detailed, physical designs has been highly beneficial to my growth as a designer. It strengthened my understanding of colour theory, typography and layout. It also developed an understanding into the intricacies of printing, such as the Pantone colour space, print preparation and sourcing a reliable printer that will get the job done on time.

I believe the final product express my personal style effectively, showing restrain within each design by making a conscious effort to only include relevant and meaningful elements. With admittedly varying success, I challenged myself to experiment with different colour schemes, helping to expand upon personal preferences for future work.
